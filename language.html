Languages in the United States

This visual represents the most active languages in the United States. The languages that are most stand-alone reflect those languages with the greatest count in the first year and in totality. Lines with greater arcs represents the greatest growth or change overall.

To aid with viewing all languages that underwent a growth in presence, the chart was formatted to a logaritmic scale, helping to normalize languages (ie. english) with the greatest of magnitudes.

The top and right most line represents English (en), followed by Italian (it) as the most active languages in the United States. We see that English (en) has a steeper growth curve, indicative of faster growth Italian-posts faster than any other language measured in the Pixstory data.

English had the greatest change when measured by increment, with a delta of 17,352 increase in posts and 5,012 increase of presence in the United States for the one year change, followed by Italian (it) as a close second. Conversely for languages that did not have a beginning value of 0, French (fr) had the greatest percent increase at over 90% for both post representation and presence in the United States, as indicated as the longest arc on the chart. Overall, this chart helped to affirm what we previously measured by providing a helpful visual representation of language performance and change in the Pixstory dataset.
