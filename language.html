Languages in the United States

This visual represents the most active languages in the United States. The languages that are most stand-alone reflect those languages with the greatest count in the first year and in totality. Lines with greater arcs represents the greatest growth or change overall.

The chart is on a logaritmic scale, the help reduce the magnitude of the most active language (english) on the chart. The x-axis represents the number of languages by date: comparing the first year's count to the total count. The y-axis is contextualized by specific language.

The top and right most line represents English (en), followed by Itlaian (it) as the most active languages in the United States. We see that Italian (it) has a steeper growth curve, indicative that it gained more Italian-posts faster (relative to its intitial count) than any other language measured in the Pixstory data.

In other words, it had the greatest percent increase of all languages, however not the greatest count, overall.
